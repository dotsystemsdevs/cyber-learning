# Integrity

![CIA Definitions](cia-definitions.png)

> The information is safe from accidental or intentional modification or alteration.

---

## What it means
Integrity means that data has not been tampered with. If someone changes, deletes or corrupts information without authorization — integrity is broken.

## Real examples
- An attacker modifies a number in a bank transfer
- A file is corrupted during transmission
- Log files are deleted to hide traces of an attack

## Attacks that break Integrity
- **Man-in-the-Middle (MitM)** — intercept and modify data in transit
- **SQL Injection** — modify database records
- **Rootkit** — alters system files to hide presence

## How it is protected
- Hashing (a hash verifies that a file has not changed)
- Digital signatures (proves data came from a trusted source)
- Audit logs (tracks who changed what and when)
